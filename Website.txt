            <h2>Summary</h2>
            <p>
                Welcome! I am an applied microeconomist specializing in econometric impact evaluations of social policies and health interventions, with a particular focus on historically minoritized communities. Specifically, I investigate how local and global programs interact with different cultural contexts to shape household choices and resource allocation. In my research, I employ quasi-experimental methods using large-scale household surveys and administrative data to empirically and causally validate existing theoretical models in the behavioral sciences. In my current role, I am undergoing postdoctoral training in experimental design, implementation science, mixed-methods analysis, biostatistics, and epidemiology.
            </p>
        </section>
